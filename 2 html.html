Few facts about Global warming

GLOBAL WARMING, the phenomenon of increasing average air temperatures near the surface of Earth over the past one to two centuries. Climate scientists have since the mid-20th century gathered detailed observations of various weather phenomena (such as temperatures, precipitation, and storms) and of related influences on climate (such as ocean currents and the atmosphere’s chemical composition). These data indicate that Earth’s climate has changed over almost every conceivable timescale since the beginning of geologic time and that human activities since at least the beginning of the Industrial Revolution have a growing influence over the pace and extent of present-day climate change.

Top 3 facts about global warming

  1. Changes in migration patters:- some animals rely on specific temperature cues to trigger their migration.
  2. Extinction risk:- many species may struggle to adapt to the rapid changes in temperature and other climate-related factors.
  3. Altered food availability:- warming oceans can cause shifts in the distribution and abundance of marine species, affecting the availability of prey for marine animals like whales,dolphins,and seabirds
